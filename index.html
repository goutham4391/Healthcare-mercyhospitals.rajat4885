ECG

ECG stands for electrocardiogram, and it measures the electrical impulses that trigger your heartbeat. You record your heartbeat with electrodes that are inserted into your chest. Their signals are sent to an electrocardiograph, which displays the results as a continuously wavy line and spikes. Mercy Hospital doctors have qualified medical staff to analyze the graph to determine the diseases.

Diagnose irregular heart rhythm (arrhythmias)

Identify structural problems with your heart’s chambers

It's safe and completely painless

Availability of ambulatory & resting ECG

2D ECHO

A 2D echocardiography test or commonly known as 2D echo is used to diagnose cardiac disorders. It is possible to observe and evaluate cardiac muscles, cardiac chambers, valves, etc. using sound waves. It is also used to determine the amount and rate of blood flow through all the chambers of the heart.

Abnormality of blood flow within the heart

Malfunctioning of the heart valve

Congenital heart diseases and blood clots or tumours

Any underlying heart diseases or abnormalities
